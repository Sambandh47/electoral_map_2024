Electoral Vote Map Manual

What is the Electoral College?
In the United States, the president is chosen by an Electoral College system. Each state is allocated a certain number of electoral votes based on its population, with a total of 538 electoral votes available across all states and Washington, D.C.

How Does It Work?
Each state is assigned electoral votes based on its population, with 270 votes needed to win. The candidate who wins the popular vote in each state typically receives all its electoral votes (exceptions: Maine and Nebraska).

Map Interpretation
Red represents states likely to vote for Trump, blue for Harris, and gray for toss-up states. The number shown on each state represents its electoral votes.

Reading the Totals
The totals for each candidate and toss-up states are shown at the top of the map. The goal is to reach the majority threshold of 270 electoral votes to win the election.
